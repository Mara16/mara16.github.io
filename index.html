About

I am a National Science Foundation GRFP Fellow and second-year PhD student in the department of Computer Science (CS) at Northwestern University (NU) in the Robert R. McCormick School of Engineering and Applied Science. As a member of the Personalized & Adaptive Technology for Health (NU-PATH) lab, my PhD work is mentored by Dr. Maia L. Jacobs.

My research interests span across the fields of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Preventive Medicine, AI and Learning Sciences, converging to explore the interplay between technology's design, personalized health tools, and innovative educational approaches. The objective of my work is to design novel strategies and interventions that apply user-centered design to address usability, safety, and ethical challanges when implementing machine learning insights into clinical workflows and similar settings.

I am currently working with Center for Advancing Safety of Machine Intelligence (CASMI) in my department to Co-Designing Patient-Facing Machine Learning for Prenatal Stress Reduction.
