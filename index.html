About

Hi, my name is Mara Ulloa. I am a first-year Computer Science PhD student at Northwestern University, where I am mentored by Maia Jacobs.

My interdisciplinary research interests include understanding how Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Artificial Intelligence (AI) can be used to mitigate disparities in healthcare. Through my work, I also explore the use of computer-supported collaborative work (CSCW) to assist the development and evaluation of machine learning technologies that have the potential to shape medical workflows. Additionally, I also have a special interest in investigating how to support learning environments for diverse groups of students. I am currently working alongside a Chicago non-profit whose mission is to introduce youth from underserved backgrounds to coding through free educational resources.
